DK English School's curriculum is organized around four pillars that prepare students to read, write, and converse fluently in a world reshaped by AI. Each pillar draws on Dr. Kim's research in language acquisition, multimodal storytelling, and formative education.

## Foundations: Academic English for the AI Era
{: .section-header}

Reading comprehension, vocabulary depth, and grammar fluency — taught with attention to how AI tools both accelerate and reshape language learning. Students learn to use English well *and* to use AI responsibly as a collaborator rather than a substitute.

## Voice & Identity through Multimodal Storytelling
{: .section-header}

Drawing on research in digital storytelling, students compose across modes — text, image, audio, video — to develop a clear voice and a sense of who they are becoming as English speakers. Bilingual identity is treated as an asset, not a hurdle.

## Critical Engagement with AI
{: .section-header}

Programs that teach students to interrogate, prompt, and evaluate generative AI outputs. Students learn fact-checking, source evaluation, and rhetorical analysis in an AI-saturated information environment — preparing them for academic settings where AI is a baseline tool, not an exception.

## Conversation, Pronunciation & Real-World Fluency
{: .section-header}

Live, low-stakes speaking practice with both human teachers and AI conversation partners. Pronunciation coaching, idiom acquisition, and confidence-building for academic, professional, and creative contexts.
